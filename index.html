FAQ – Solar Panel Cleaning & Savings

Helpful answers about why cleaning matters, how often to do it, and how much extra energy clean panels can produce.

Why should I clean my solar panels?

Over time, dust, pollen, ash, bird droppings, and pollution build up on the glass. That layer blocks sunlight and reduces how much power your system can produce. A proper cleaning restores clarity so your panels can do the job you paid for.

How much more energy can clean panels produce?

Most studies and field tests show production losses of 5–20% on dirty residential systems, and sometimes more on especially dusty or flat roofs. Even a 10% boost can add up to hundreds of dollars in extra power over the life of your system.

How often should I have my panels cleaned in the Denver area?

For most Denver Metro & Front Range homes, we recommend a professional cleaning once or twice per year. If you’re near construction, dirt roads, heavy trees, wildfires, or lots of birds, you may benefit from more frequent cleanings.

Can I clean my solar panels myself with a hose or pressure washer?

We don’t recommend it. Hard water, harsh brushes, or high pressure can leave mineral spots, scratch the glass, or damage seals and wiring—potentially affecting your warranty. We use pure deionized water and panel-safe tools designed for solar.

Will cleaning void my solar panel warranty?

No—our method is specifically designed to be warranty-friendly. We avoid chemicals, abrasives, and pressure washing. We use pure water, soft brushes, and gentle rinse techniques that align with most manufacturers’ care guidelines.

How do I know if cleaning made a difference?

You’ll get before/after photos of your panels, and we can help you compare your system monitoring before and after the cleaning. Many homeowners notice a visible bump in their daily kWh production, especially if the panels were heavily soiled.
